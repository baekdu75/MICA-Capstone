The Myth of "EVs Are Always Cheaper"

Electric vehicles are often marketed as universally cheaper to operate than gas-powered cars. The pitch is simple and appealing: electricity costs less than gasoline, so you'll save money every time you drive.

But this narrative glosses over a critical truth: the real cost depends heavily on where you live. A single national average doesn't capture the dramatic differences in electricity and gasoline prices across the United States. What's cheap in one state might be expensive in another.

2

Energy Costs Vary Wildly by State

When you look at the data, one pattern becomes immediately clear: electricity and gas prices change dramatically across state lines.
